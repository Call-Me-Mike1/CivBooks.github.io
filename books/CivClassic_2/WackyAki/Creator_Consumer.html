If we were to go back in time a few hundred years, the world would, obviously, look a lot different. Aside from technological and intellectual differences, one of the core elements that past human society posessed that has been lost is the relationship between one and ones work.
Page 2 of 13
Take, for example, a tomato. Today, we drive to the grocery store, buy a tomato, go through a self checkout and then take it home to eat and use. A few hundred years ago, should you be lucky enough to have tomato's to eat, you would only be able to buy it from a farmer

who you would have known your whole life, and you would have eaten the tomato at home knowing that he or she fed you and you paid them for the food, providing their income. The major difference here is that our modern day society doesn't have that degree of relationship. Today,

we are completely and utterly divorced from the producer and consumer. When you work to create, you have no idea who is consuming, and vice versa, creating an inevidable divide between these two groups. The divide is, ultimately, going to tear apart the western world.

How much can you possibly care for people's struggles if you don't interact with them? How can you know a poor fast food worker's problems if the extent of your interaction with them is through the drive-thru window? While it starts on issues like Healthcare, it will quickly expand

to impact every single issue that modern society faces. All because we don't have a platform and way to have a constructive dialogue with those who's material conditions are different from our own. Access to education, for example, is greatly reduced for the

poor compared to the rich and middle class, and while a few hundred years ago the daily interactions we had would have revealed this disparagement, nowadays the divide brances this issue. This is a simple byproduct of several conscious choices.
