The ideal government is something political theorists, philosophers, and average citizens have sought after for millennia, with three major ideologies being manufactured, these being liberalism, conservatism, and centrism.
Page 3 of 21
§lLiberalism §0Liberalism is the idea that morality, laws, and primary beliefs are inherently a construct, and can be malleable to the beholder. This belief system is held by most of the world, with popularity reaching its fullest since the early 1990s.
Page 4 of 21
§lConservatism §0Conservatism is the opposite of liberalism, with the belief that morality, laws, and primary beliefs are grounded and cannot be changed. This belief system is the second most popular, with popularity started to rise since the 1960-80s.
Page 5 of 21
§lCentrism §0Centrism is the middle ground between conservatism and liberalism, with the ultimate goal of being unbiased necessary to an ideal government. Secondly, centrism is one of the least popular belief systems, with it being hated by many others.
Page 6 of 21
In order to properly create an ideal government, analyzation of the major types of governmental rule must be used. Two examples will be shown, democracy and totalitarianism. Firstly, the analyzation of democracy shall be employed.
Page 7 of 21
§lDemocracy §0 Democracy is the most used governmental system in the world, with a majority of the world using it as the basis for government. Democracy can be divided into two major types, these being federalism and unitarism.
Page 8 of 21
§lFederalism §0Federalism is a type of democracy where the state is divided into substates, such as provinces or states. This system also divides itself in twain, with cooperative and dual federalism. A mixture between the two can be used.
Page 9 of 21
A summary of these two types is needed. Cooperative federalism allows the national (also known as federal) and state governments to "cooperate" on issues the state government cannot handle on its own. Examples of this include healthcare, military, and education.
Page 10 of 21
Secondly, dual federalism separates the national and state governments. This has been regarded as the true form of federalism. National law triumphs state law in most cases; however, if state nullification is legal, the state wields the right to "nullify" the federal law.
Page 11 of 21
§lUnitarism §0Unitarism is the opposite of federalism, with all power going towards the national government. This belief system is used throughout the European Union, albeit not being used by the EU itself.
Page 12 of 21
§lTotalitarianism §0Totalitarianism is the opposite of democracy, with power going to the govt. instead of the people. Totalitarianism also involves the government's control of absolutely everything in a person's life.
Page 13 of 21
This is widely regarded as one of the worst govermental systems, as any form of personal freedom is warped to serving the government, instead of themselves. Totalitarianism also employs Marxist-Leninist Communism to further destroy freedom.

Population: 30+ Area: 1000 sq. blks Instant dual federalism, as a population and area of this size would warrent completely provincial/state control in their own regions, with the power to nullify federal laws being their greatest assets.
