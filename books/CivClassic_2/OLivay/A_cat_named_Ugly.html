Page 2 of 21
Everyone in the aparment complex I live in knew who Ugly was. Ugly was the resident tomcat. Ugly loved three things in this world: fighting, eating garbage, and shall we say, Love.
Page 3 of 21
The combination of these things combined with a life spent outside had their effect on Ugly. To start with, he had only one eye, and where the other should have been was a gaping hole.
Page 4 of 21
He was also missing his ear on the same side, his left foot has appeared to have been badly broken at one time, and had healed at an unnatural angle, making him look like he was always turning the corner.
Page 5 of 21
His tail has long since been lost, leaving only the smallest stub, which he would constantly jerk and twitch.
Page 6 of 21
Ugly would have been a dark gray tabby striped-type, except for the sores covering his head, neck, even his shoulders with thick, yellowing scabs.
Page 7 of 21
Every time someone saw Ugly there was the same reaction. "That's one UGLY cat!!" All the children were warned not to touch him.
Page 8 of 21
The adults threw rocks at him, hosed him down, squirted him when he tried to come in their homes, or shut his paws in the door when he would not leave.
Page 9 of 21
Ugly always had the same reaction. If you turned the hose on him, he would stand there, getting soaked until you gave up and quit. If you threw things at him, he would curl his lanky body around feet in forgivness.
Page 10 of 21
Whenever he spied children, he would come running, meowing frantically and bump his head against their hands, begging for their love. If you ever picked him up he would immediatly begin suckling on your shirt, earring, whatever he could find.
Page 11 of 21
One day Ugly shared his love with the neighbor's huskies. They did not respond kindly, and Ugly was badly mauled. From my aparment I could hear his scream, and I tried to rush to his aid.
Page 12 of 21
By the time I got to where he was laying, it was apparent Ugly's life was almost at an end.§0 §0 §0Ugly lay in a wet circle, his back legs and lower back twisted grossly out of shape,
Page 13 of 21
a gaping tear in the white strip of fur that ran down his front.§0 §0 §0As I picked him up and tried to carry him home I could hear him wheezing and gasping, and could feel him struggling. I must be hurting him terribly I thought.
Page 14 of 21
Then I felt a familiar tugging, sucking sensation on my ear - Ugly, in so much pain, suffering and obviously dying was trying to suckle my ear.
Page 15 of 21
I pulled him closer to me, and he bumped the palm of my hand with his head, then he turned his one golden eye towards me, and I could hear the distinct sound of purring.
Page 16 of 21
Even in the greatest pain, that ugly battled-scarred cat was asking for a little affection, perhaps some compassion.§0 §0 §0At that moment I thought Ugly was the most beautiful, loving creature I had ever seen.
Page 17 of 21
Never once did he try to bite or scratch me, or even try to get away from me, or struggle in any way. Ugly just looked up at me, completely trusting in me to relieve his pain.
Page 18 of 21
Ugly died in my arms before I could get inside, but I sat and held him for a long time afterwards, thinking about how one scarred, deformed little stray could so alter my opinion about what is means to have true pureness of spirit,
Page 19 of 21
to love so totally and truly. Ugly taught me more about giving and compassion than a thousand books, lectures, or talk show specials ever could, and for that I will always be thankful.
Page 20 of 21
He had been scarred on the outside, but I was scarred on the inside, and it was time for me to move on and learn to love truly and deeply. To give my total to those I cared for.
Page 21 of 21
Many people want to be richer, more successful, well liked, beautiful, but for me, I will always try to be Ugly.
