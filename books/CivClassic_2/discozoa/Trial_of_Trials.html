Though the Bog Knight endures much turmoil and hardship, this day did not present such things. Instead, the Bog Knight was transported northwards by a mysterious and unfamiliar force. Looking hither and thither, he found himself in Augsburg, territory of the
Page 2 of 9
Senatus Populusque Romanus. Remembering from a previous northern expecdition, he began his trek southwards. Crossing a river, he found an isle of Gensokyo and needing a rest, settled. There he created the Hut of the Lost and lamented his situation. Still, he embarked southwards.
Page 3 of 9
The next land he came across was the so called Tvtopia, where strange gliphs and truly gargantuan artwork was displayed. He marvelled at the great structures, yet he continued southwards. The Bog Knight knew that directly south of this land would be he Truidencian mainland.
Page 4 of 9
With this knowledge, he set across the sea to land at the Imperial Bay, or so he thought. Instead he had landed on the shores of Icenia. Ignorant of this truth, he pressed onwards. On his way, he found a massive vault. Curiously, he went to investigate, but from the aether the voice of
Page 5 of 9
Obtainable Spatula told the fool that he was in Icenian land and to return eastwards or face dangers that he could not aid him in. Across the Concord River, he was on his way home. These lands had become familiar to him and he lowered his guard. All the way back to his fair island

of Inis Moin, he thought that his troubles had ceased. Unbeknownst to him, a fiendish hooligan had set his abode ablaze. As soon as he entered, he was consumed by flame. Now returned to that strange land of Romans in the north, he was peeved. Now knowing the path home,

he rushed southward as soon as possible. By Tvtopia, Imperial Bay and the Truidencian Sea, he returned and extinguished the flames, only to find his maps, many valuables and bed gone. At this sight he was demoralized significantly, yet knew that he had to push

onwards and perservere. Across the isle he searched and there it was, the fiend's camp. Retaking his things and sumerging the pitiful camp in magma, the Bog Knight could rest. Though this trip had not been of his own will, he still knew that it must have been necessary.
