===================THE BOX THEATRE§0 §0=================== A reasonable start to any architectural examination of Rotterdam, the BOX Theatre is the anchor point from which the modern city has grown. A stone mountain whose size is further exaggerated by the
Page 9 of 50
relative flatness of plazas and open lots around it, the BOX Theatre was not Rotterdam's first construction, but it was the first build that matters, and is still around today. That last point though has been occasionally controversial throughout
Page 10 of 50
Columbia's history, as the BOX Theatre is a jagged, rocky, new mountain. Built out of essentially one material, smoothstone brick, it is a cross between an Austrian Operahouse and Dracula's Castle. Like the early Columbia it was built in, it is a flurry
Page 11 of 50
of ideas, with gargantuan statues, an elegant stone trim, a sloped roof, beautiful windows, stout collumns, and hanging lanterns, it is proof that the whole is not always greater than the sum of its parts. Still, with chaos come character, and who can
Page 12 of 50
truly admonish a build that was created collectively, as a birthday present to another citizen, with great optimism for the Columbia to come. Who can talk badly about a building with such heart, such history.§0 §0

===================Rotterdam Town Hall§0 §0===================I find it remarkable that despite the fact that I have lived in Columbia nearly two years, despite all monuments and great works I have completed, that I can still be amazed at the beauty of our
Page 14 of 50
Town Hall. Just to the North of the Plaza of the Republic and adjacent to the BOX Theatre, this is another structure that hails from the early days of Columbia, and in my mind represents a time when Columbia began setting a course for success. I find it
Page 15 of 50
to be a wonderful build, both simple and complex in the right places. Just over three stories with a sloped roof and central flag tower, from the rooftops it has the appearance of a University building or a Schoolhouse, or maybe even a Town Hall, which in
Page 16 of 50
my book is a success. It represents what it is to a T, with its modest two wings attached to a central atrium, correclty wide and correctly long, and always bright on the outside due to the sea lamps. The building has seen recent completion this past year
Page 17 of 50
thanks to a comprehensive remodel by the author of the interior spaces. While an exceptional exterior, once one entered the building one had the dissapointing fact displayed in their face that 50% of the structure was dedicated to a giant stairwell, and
Page 18 of 50
as part of early 2019 modernization efforts by Columbia, the inside was completely gutted and a new interior installed, with colorful and rich offices, meeting rooms, archives, and even a small post office. An exceptional rebirth to an exceptional
Page 19 of 50
structure, and the embodiement of the Columbian Phoenix.

===================KNOT Tower§0 §0===================What if a building served as an identity? What if you were to spend months on a building, a single structure, and not some vault hole or mindless cavern, but a legitimate and functional building. What would
Page 21 of 50
it look like? The answer for one such player can be seen in Knot Tower, by KnotNSA, a cultural fact of the city that is rich in it's art deco design. Like many minecraft skyscrapers, its beauty comes in the repetition of its facade, and this can be seen
Page 22 of 50
in the first few stories of the tower, but as you crane your neck towards the spire, the building diminishes in a wonderful mathematical subtraction, until terminating in an antenna. The exterior is reminiscent of the best parts of New York City at the
Page 23 of 50
turn of the 19th century into the 20th, and one can imagine elements of the Chrysler Building, Empire State Building, perhaps the Plaza Hotel as well. §0 §0 §0The true victory of the tower over Rotterdam comes infact from its insides however, which have

seen numerous rennovations through the course of Columbian history, and as a result offer an enjoyable experience. Still, when you turn away from the robust detailing of the interior, you can sometimes still see dark corners that have remained unfinished
Page 25 of 50
and forgotten for years.

===================Plamen Plaza§0 §0===================A simple building that does nothing more than fit in with its surroundings, it was built competently but without any spirit or motivation, no confidence. The insides however have experienced a relatively
Page 27 of 50
recent rejuvination, and is home to the Columbian History Museum. Odds and ends from the past are displayed and proof of the rich history of the nation; interactive exhibits allow a fun five minute romp. Still, one can't be blamed for not giving the
Page 28 of 50
building a second look and assuming it to be another bland apartment block.

===================Grand Hotel Rotterdam§0 §0===================If you peaked between some of the more elegant structures lining Imperial Boulevard, standing proudly behind some of them would be the Grand Hotel Rotterdam. A fun structure that not only
Page 30 of 50
matches the style of the town, but goes one step further by providing an elegant roof and solid use of green, this tower is a welcome anchor inside and out. Its lobby features one of two of Rotterdam's indoor pools, and its rooms exude luxury despite
Page 31 of 50
being relatively compact. The building is wider than it is longer, which is a benefit, as a glance to its sides can sometimes be dissapointing as they are mostly lack of any solid detail. The author recommends spending some time on the second-floor garden
Page 32 of 50
, as it was his favorite feature to install in the building.

===================Rotterdam Central Train Terminus§0 §0===================What if you were tasked with creating your country's premier international arrivals terminal in what amounted to a one-chunk 70 block deep hole? The author had to face this problem
Page 34 of 50
in constructing Rotterdam Station, which today is a mixed victory, like much of Columbian history. It was built during a period of wavering Columbian prosperity, and as a result its features are sometimes explosive and sometimes bland. Its scale is its
Page 35 of 50
strength, as the platforms level is cavernous and cues you in to the monumental feeling you will feel throughout a visit to Rotterdam. Its weakness is its layout - because of the aforementioned issue of being built in a deep hole, with tracks at the
Page 36 of 50
bottom, transiting the station involves utilizing extensive stairs, which are beautiful and spacious but also sometimes tiresome. The station was exhausting to build, and is proving exhausting to write about. Thank god its finally done.

===================The Kdeeks Memorial Bridge§0 §0===================The first monument of infrastructure in Rotterdam, but certainly not the last, this suspension bridge connects two highland farm villages, and for a long time towered over the northern areas
Page 38 of 50
of the city. Its relevancy has waxed and waned like its namesake, and it sometimes sees heavy use, or none at all. Still, proof that measured simplicity in infrastructure can be eye-pleasing, this bridge is as solid as its foundations.

===================Kdeeks' Mansion (Royalist Party HQ)§0 §0===================Royalism may be controversial, but this building is not. A wonderful addition to the East side of town, The Kdeeks Mansion has beautiful gardens, an elegant Second-Empire roof,
Page 40 of 50
wonderful interiors, and is simultaneously not too small and not too large. If you can get an invite to the exclusive but luxurious insides, do not pass it up, the door opens for few but it holds back a different world of distinction and dignity.
