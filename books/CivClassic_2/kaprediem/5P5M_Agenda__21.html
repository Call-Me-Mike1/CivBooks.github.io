THE FIVE-POINT and FIVE-MONTH AGENDA by President Kaprediem Republic of Lambat
Page 2 of 11
In order to attain a bright and prosperous future, Lambat must take a direction that leaves no aspect of the nation behind. It currently faces problems such as low productivity and low immigration.
Page 3 of 11
What I'm aiming is for Lambat to become a regional leader in economic and cultural development within the next ten months. Therefore, I present this five-point, ten-month agenda.
Page 4 of 11
Economy -Boost agricultural and mining output to self-sufficient levels through comprehensive land reform. -Open accessible channels for free trade primarily through establishing duty-free zones.
Page 5 of 11
-Homesteading reform. Create attractive incentives for newfriends and oldfriends in order to promote settlement in Lambat. -Launch a massive transportation infrastructure program to facilitate the efficient movement of people and goods.
Page 6 of 11
Diplomacy -Uphold protective neutrality within international affairs. -Invite foreign delegates for meaningful economic and cultural exchanges.
Page 7 of 11
-Set up relevant economic and cultural programs to promote international goodwill. -Visit other nations in order to establish diplomatic relations.
Page 8 of 11
Defense -Maintaining the renunciation of aggression as a means of achieving national goals. -Strengthening the defense capabilities of Lambat. -Establishing volunteer armed forces and a foreign legion.
Page 9 of 11
Culture -Foster the development of a unique Lambatican cultural identity through the arts. -Uphold the freedom of expression and its responsibilities.
Page 10 of 11
Administration -Attract participation from ordinary citizens in public affairs. -Eventual transition to a full-fledged liberal democracy with powers shared among co-equal branches of government. ###
