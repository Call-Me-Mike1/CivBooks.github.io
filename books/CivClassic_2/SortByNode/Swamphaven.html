Back in the winter of 2015/2016, many of the long-time members of this server lived§0 §0in MegaC and the surrounding areas.§0 §0At this time, a looter/PvPer named KingPacha snuck into town by using an alt account and getting invited.
Page 4 of 19
After getting the lay of the land and waiting for all the players to get offline, he struck, destroying beds, looting chests, burning homes, and griefing anything that doesn't burn.
Page 5 of 19
Some players rose to the challenge and fought back. He was killed once, but it was mostly a lost cause. He would respawn nearby and continue his rampant destruction through three different accounts.
Page 6 of 19
Many people fled to a bunker within walking distance. However, this plan was foiled when a shared map led KingPacha right to the hideout. Because the bunker was compromised, many performed a group suicide to return to spawn. Thus, Vault 420 was born.
Page 7 of 19
Vault 420 was a hideout near spawn.§0 §0It was meant to be a place to regroup for those who were sent to spawn and who needed to restrengthen before heading out.
Page 8 of 19
It was inhabited by around 8 people and any refugees from MegaC were invited (though they didn't necessarily stay long). Being a§0 §0secret fort, it was underground and§0 §0had no evidence of its existence above-ground.
Page 9 of 19
This aspect made it undesirable --§0 §0people wanted to live somewhere they could build. Plans were first made to build a giant nether pathway to a new site. However, this ran the risk of having a path leading right toward the new town.
Page 10 of 19
So, instead, the coalition headed to the nether rooftop for undetectable travel. The agreement was that the town would be built wherever the portal lead the team -- a swamp.
Page 11 of 19
Swamphaven was built on stilts over the shifting swamp mud. The town consisted of boardwalks, lamps, and pirate flags.§0 §0It was a bonafide tinderbox, ready to be destroyed in one shot by a griefer. Thus, it had to remain completely secret.
Page 12 of 19
The only known way to access it was by following a map on the nether roof. This resulted in residents being unable to return without an escort, but it was a price to pay for secrecy.
Page 13 of 19
During the town's existence, a member of the server decided to declare himself the owner of a beloved town, Sealand. This infuriated many members of the server -- including members of Swamphaven.
Page 14 of 19
Many returned to spawn to pick a fight. One found himself in a rough battle with a map to Swamphaven in his inventory. Upon dying, the safety of Swamphaven was crushed. The city could be visited at any moment and burned to the ground.
Page 15 of 19
It was time to pack up the city. Several members fled to the surrounding regions. Some joined a PvP clan lead by a server member. In the end, most stopped playing. Truly, it is a tale of the punishment griefing can dole out.
