Section 1- Executive Power
Page 6 of 20
The Nation of Pandoria shall be led by a small group of 2 consuls. These consuls are elected officials, being elected once per month. Powers of these consuls include day to day activities (production of buildings, city planning, allocation of resources,
Page 7 of 20
etc), being chief diplomats in international representations, and proposing legislation, along with plans for war to the remaining governing body to vote on. Furthermore, the consuls can appoint different chair members, representing different departments
Page 8 of 20
of the nation as they see fit. The consuls can be replaced with a unanimous vote among the governing body if and only if a consul is proven to be overstepping powers in a judicial trial. After ratification, the Consuls, along with their chair members have
Page 9 of 20
the power to amend the Constitution of Pandoria.§0

Section 2- Legislative§0 §0Power
Page 11 of 20
The state of Pandoria, along with its legislation lies in the power of the people, and the government. For smaller issues, deemed fit as small by the government, both the consuls and appointed chair positions will democratically vote on issues. For issues
Page 12 of 20
deemed larger and more significant by the government as a whole, a direct vote from all of the populace will be held to decide the outcomes of certain issues. The direct elections, along with the chair members provide a system of checks and balances for
Page 13 of 20
the consuls, to prevent consuls from growing too powerful. Furthermore, the chair members will be responsible for overseeing different aspects of the nation, specifically aspects dealing with their department. The chair members shall include the positions
Page 14 of 20
of Foreign minister, interior minister, and industrial minister.

Section 3- Judicial§0
Page 16 of 20
A single judiciary member shall oversee and supervise all elections and cases given to them. The judiciary member will act as a mediator in intrastate issues between the government and people. Furthermore, the judiciary official still can vote on these
Page 17 of 20
issues. The judiciary can be appointed by the consuls, however, not removed by them. The removal of the judiciary, along with any governmental official shall lie in the results of a direct vote from all of the populace.§0
