Preamble§0 §0The people of Freelands believe that it is the sovereign duty of the citizen to preserve liberty and self-determination. These values are the pillars that uphold our society and protect the freedoms of its people.
Page 3 of 20
Article I§0 §0The governor of Freelands is to be elected by popular vote in regular elections. After the governor is chosen, he shall appoint one or two senators to carry out the duties of the state.
Page 4 of 20
Article II§0 §0The provincial court of Freelands is beneath the courts of the Entranan Republic. The provincial courts shall settle minor matters with penalties that do not exceed a pearling of two weeks, banishment, or execution.
Page 5 of 20
Article III§0 §0The citizens of the province may call for a referendum at any time to reverse or enact a law, or to impeach a sitting senator or governor. Upon impeachment, either a new senator shall be chosen or a new governor elected.
Page 6 of 20
Article IV§0 §0The senators may propose legislation, but this legislation must be approved by the governor and the citizens. A simple majority is needed for the citizen vote. The governor may veto the legislation, however vetoeing will trigger a referendum.
Page 7 of 20
Article V§0 §0If the national government should ever cease to exist, Freelands will declare independence as a sovereign state. If this occurs, the senate and governor will gain the ability to declare war, which requires a unanimous vote.
Page 8 of 20
Article VI§0 §0The province is under no obligation to extradite or hand over criminals unless the governor receives a direct order from the national government.
Page 9 of 20
Article VII§0 §0The provincal government is allowed to impose minor regulations on trade, maintain a militia to enforce the law, establish a postal service, create public works, and manage public lands. The senators will oversee any related bureaucracies.
Page 10 of 20
Article VIII§0 §0The provincial government has the power to declare martial law at any time. In such an event, the governor takes control of the militia and rights may be suspended. The government may also seize property deemed necessary to the state.
Page 11 of 20
Article IX§0 §0The Entranan Republic is the supreme ruler of the land. The province is subordinate to the national government and the national government has the final say in any domestic matters.
